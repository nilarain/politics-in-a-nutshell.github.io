Immigration

Extend your knowledge on why the nation of immigrants is always caught up in debate.

Summary

Immigration is the international movement of foreign people into a country they are not native. Immigrants choose to come to the US, or other first world country for various reasons, commonly to live in freedom, to escape oppression or poverty, and to make a better lives for themselves and their family.


There are over 40 million immigrants living in America today. Over 11 million are undocumented and account for 13 percent of the total US population and about 16 percent of the labor force. Most of these immigrants originate from Mexico and Central America. Immigration reform is primarily focusing on improving the effiency of the system, for both legal and illegal immigrants in the United States. Mainly Republicans are against immigration reform. Current Policy

  • Immigrants suspected of terrorism, violent criminals, gang members and recently border crossers are at the top of the deportation priority list.
  • Since the 1990s, the U.S Border Patrol’s budget has increased by 714 percent and there is strong focus on increasing resources for border enforcement.
  • Anyone attempting to cross the border illegally will be caught and sent back.
  • Children who came to the U.S. before the age of 16 and have lived continuously since January 1st 2010 can receive deferred action, so they will be able to stay in the U.S. temporarily and will be considered for employment authorization.
  • Spouses and children of Legal Permanent Residents are able to apply for a waiver from within the U.S.
  • Parents of a U.S. citizen or of a Legal Permanent Resident, who have lived in the U.S. continuously since January 1st 2010, are eligible to apply for Deferred Action for Parental Accountability and a work permit, if they pass a background check.
  • Low-skilled guest workers can apply for a W-visa, which, however, is not a step towards applying for permanent residency or citizenship.
  • Employers can bring foreign workers for seasonal agricultural work. H-2A visa holders have the same workers’ rights as American workers. The document is valid for 364 days.
  • Undocumented and low-income immigrant children in California will receive health insurance through the state’s Medicaid program.
  • Based on President Obama’s executive actions, illegals could file and claim refunds for the last three years under the Earned Income Tax Credit.

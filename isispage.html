History of ISIS

The story of ISIS began with the invasion of Afghanistan in 1979 by the Soviet Union, which ended in 1989. Many young Afghan men joined to fight what they felt was a religious struggle against the Soviets. To protect Islam, Al Qaeda, led by Osama bin Laden, and ISIS, led by Abu Musab Al-Zarqawi, formed. In spite of their common cause, the two did not get along. During 2001, Al Qaeda attacked the U.S. in a series of coordinated terrorist attacks known as 9/11. Afterwards, Bin Laden left for Pakistan, while Zarqawi remained on the down low in Iraq.



The September attack on the World Trade Center.


In response to 9/11, the U.S. declared war and attacked the dictatorship of Saddam in Iraq in 2003. This maneuver disbanded the Iraqi army, and many began joining Zarqawi, including Jihadists. In 2004, Al Qaeda and ISIS joined forces. A U.S.-led airstrike killed Zarqawi, and for a while, Iraq was stable.

During 2011, protesters across the Middle East began to rise in what became known as the Arab Spring. The government of Syria led by the dictator, Assad, clashed with the people, and Assad unleashed extremist jihadists from prisons in a move against the protesters, instigating a civil war. Zarqawi’s remaining followers united with the protesters under the leadership of Abu Bakr al-Baghdadi, who let loose jihadists from prisons in Iraq. He sent Jabhat Al Nusra to Syria to expand, naming his group ISIS (Islamic State of Iraq and Syria). Nusra, a member of Al Qaeda, rejected Baghdadi’s ideals, foreshadowing violence between Al Qaeda and ISIS. However, the dictator of Syria, Assad, supports ISIS since the group blocks out foreigners.

Refugee Crisis

These human beings are just like you and me; They don’t wake up with the song of the bird or the sounds of a car but with screams, bombs, and desperation. If you’re fearing for your life every second of your existence, will you not seek means to improve them?

A refugee person who has been forced to leave their country in order to escape war, persecution, or natural disaster.

The Facts

  • 65 million people are forcibly displaced due to conflicts within their countries
  • EU countries offered asylum to 292,540 refugees
  • The US plans to admit 10,000 Syrian refugees
  • The state of Illinois has allowed 169 but it has temporarily stopped accepting refugees
  • 50% of refugees are under the age of 18
  • Refugees make 0.19% of the UK population




Famous Refugees

  • Bob Marley (fled Jamaica to Miami due to violence)
  • Sigmund Freud (fled Nazi Austria to escape Jewish persecution)
  • Alina Fernandez (daughter of Fidel Castro, fled from Cuba to Spain)

What's going on in China?

Find out more on why China seems to be a common topic amongst discussions of educational/economic rank of the US.

Is China ruling the world?

China is on the road to global dominance, which could have consequences for the United States. It's been said many times before that China's main advantage economically is the lower cost of production, but now they are turning to improving education to produce high quality goods and services.

China's New Focus

China’s education is the largest education system in the world, accounting for 9.4 million students who took the China’s National Higher Education Entrance Examinations in June 2016. Since 1998, China has invested in “a massive expansion of education, nearly tripling the share of GDP devoted to it. A decade later, the number of colleges and students has doubled, students going from 1 million in 1997 to 5.5 million in 2007." Clearly, after building up infrastructure for years, China has been pouring funds into higher education of all forms for their citizens. Although China has now focused on education, the country still dominates in trade. If all imports and exports of goods are totalled, China beats the U.S as the biggest trading nation. Since 2007, China has become the United States’ principal source of imports accounting for 19% of total imports in 2009 outpacing Canada and Mexico that provided 14% and 11% of total imports respectively.

China's Government

A few facts about human rights in China:

  • No laws protecting LGBTQ+ people from desicrimination, some areas still enforce conversion therapy
  • Disabled children are barred from going to school, forced to be institutionalized as punishment
  • Women have very limited access to reporoductive care and many undergo forced abortion to fulfill China's population planning
  • Although freedom of religion is supposedly guaranteed, the government only recognized 5 religions and calls any other an "evil cult"
  • The press and Internet is under censorship and the government has full control of the media
  • The government has been intolerant of activists and protesters, even peaceful protests (such as in Tibet)

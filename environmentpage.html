Climate Change and Environmental Health

Chimpanzees, gorillas, orangutans have been living for hundreds of thousands of years in their forest, living fantastic lives, never destroying the forest. I would say that they have been in a way more successful than us as far as being in harmony with the environment. - Jane Goodall

Paris Agreement

On December 2015, 195 countries gathered at the Paris Climate Conference to create a plan to address the 2 degree fahrenheit increase due to climatic changes. Developed countries, such as the US, will lead and influence developing countries to decrease their carbon emissions, as well as their own, for the sake of human well being.

The Facts

  • 2014 has been the hottest year recorded.
  • The US contributes 22% to world carbon emissions.
  • Temperatures will increase 0.2% per decade.
  • Climate change could increase food prices by 50-60 percent by 2030.
  • By 2015, the number of people affected by climate change disasters could reach 375 million per year.
  • The Golden Toad was the first extinction blamed on human made global warming.

A few important pieces of legislation on the environement are the Clean Power Plan (to deliver better air quality, improve public health, and enforce clean energy investments and jobs across the country) and the Montreal Protocol (which will phase down HFCs; a potent greenhouse gas).

Gun Violence

Guns are more than a large problem: they're a lethal problem.

The Facts

Every year in the US, around 100,000 people are shot by a gun. Every day, around 280 people are shot by a gun. Nearly three times more kids (15,576) were injured by firearms in 2010 than the number of U.S. soldiers (5,247) wounded in action that year in the war in Afghanistan.

NRA


Charlton Heston, former President of the NRA, a powerful pro-gun lobbying group, best described the organization's purpose as the protection of the constitutional right to bear arms in the Second Amendment.

Regardless of the outcome of the political battles over gun control, rest assured the National Rifle Association will not rest easy until it has secured that right beyond any doubt whatsoever.


Democratic Party


The Democratic Party wants to protect Americans’ Second Amendment right to own firearms and will keep guns out of the hands of criminals and terrorists by fighting gun crime. To prevent those with malicious intentions from obtaining guns so easily, they plan to reauthorize the assault weapons ban.

Democrats want to continue strengthening gun control to better protect and reduce violence. They also wish to mandate child safety locks, photo license I.D.s for guns, background checks, and gun safety tests to buy a new handgun.


Republican Party


Republicans acknowledge, support, and defend the law-abiding citizens’ rights to self-defense. They also support federal legislation that would allow those with state-issued carry permits to carry firearms in any state that allows such permits.

Through programs like Project Exile, they will hold criminals individually accountable for their actions. They will promote strong enforcement of federal and state firearm laws. especially when guns are used in violent or drug-related crimes.

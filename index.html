WHO WE ARE / THE PROBLEM

We are Politics in a Nutshell, and to put it in a nutshell, we’re dedicated to raising political awareness in youth. A problem facing our society today is the lack of concise news directed at teenagers. Currently our news is directed to adults with a high level of vocabulary and understanding of both past and current events. Teens frequently hear their parents talking about the news, but many lack the context to understand the significance of current events.

WHY IT’S IMPORTANT / SOLUTION

The phrase “The youth is the hope of our future” is our motto here at Politics in a Nutshell. George Bernard Shaw and Robert Kennedy stated that “[the youth sees] the world as it is and ask “Why?” and imagine a different world and ask “Why not?”. Regardless of the era, this question of “Why not?” will be critical for millennials to stop taking what they hear at face value. Young people look at the world with fresh eyes and can offer a unique solutions to modern day issues that leave older generations scratching their heads. The importance of this cause is to inform teenagers who don’t know where to begin learn about policies they might be voting on and give teenagers/young adults a more digestible source on foreign and domestic policy. The youth is the hope of our future and creating a site for them to access direct information on current events would allow them to be more well-rounded adults and change the world.

THE FUTURE OF POLITICS IN A NUTSHELL

Politics in A Nutshell is at the beginning of its career, but it is never too early to start thinking of the future. We at Politics in A Nutshell will continue to expand the website to build a community and have meaningful discussions. Politics in A Nutshell's goal in the future is to make the youth politically aware ubiquitously, stand up, speak out and ask themselves, “Who am I and what can I do?”

THE BEGINNING OF POLITICS IN A NUTSHELL

Politics in a Nutshell was created and founded by five high school students from Illinois and Wisconsin who met through Girls Who Code (@ Accenture), a non profit organization trying the close the gender gap in technology. We cultivated our love for coding, learning and politics to create a website (with HTML, CSS, and Javascript) to raise political awareness in youth for our final project. This final project was created in less than two weeks.
